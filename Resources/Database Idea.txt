# AI-First HR Startup – Database Design

## 1. Users

id (uuid, PK)
name
email (unique)
role (HR | INTERVIEWER | CANDIDATE)
createdAt

---

## 2. Candidates

id (uuid, PK)
userId (FK Users)
resumeUrl
resumeText
status (applied | shortlisted | scheduled | rejected)
assignedRequirementId (nullable FK)
createdAt

---

## 3. AIProfiles

id (uuid, PK)
candidateId (FK Candidates)
experienceYears
seniorityLevel
extractedSkills (jsonb)
inferredSkills (jsonb)
strengths
weaknesses
matchScore
hireProbability
rawResponse (jsonb)
createdAt

---

## 4. TechRequirements

id (uuid, PK)
title
techStack (jsonb)
minExperience
matchThreshold
openings
createdByHRId (FK Users)
createdAt

---

## 5. AvailabilitySlots

id (uuid, PK)
userId (FK Users)
startTime (timestamp)
endTime (timestamp)
isBooked (boolean default false)
createdAt

---

## 6. Interviews

id (uuid, PK)
candidateId (FK Candidates)
interviewerId (FK Users)
hrId (FK Users)
startTime
endTime
meetLink
status (scheduled | completed | cancelled)
feedback (text nullable)
aiSummary (text nullable)
createdAt

Unique Constraints:
- (interviewerId, startTime)
- (candidateId, startTime)

---

## 7. AuditLogs

id (uuid, PK)
actorId (FK Users)
action
entityType
entityId
timestamp

---

## Indexing Strategy

Index:
- Candidates.status
- AIProfiles.matchScore
- TechRequirements.techStack (GIN)
- AvailabilitySlots.userId
- Interviews.startTime

---

## Scaling Readiness

- JSONB for AI flexibility
- Separate AIProfiles for extensibility
- Soft deletes for auditability
- Foreign key cascades disabled for safety
